Northforge / Partnering with Primes & Industry

WE PARTNER WITH PRIMES. WHERE IT SERVES THE MISSION.

Northforge operates as a specialist subcontractor within Defence and Federal Government prime consortia — bringing sovereign-cleared technical depth where it’s needed, without competing for prime status.

Our position
SPECIALIST. NOT COMPETITIVE.

Northforge is not a prime. We do not seek to be the prime on Defence and Government programmes.

What we are is a specialist sovereign-cleared partner that brings classified-environment depth and Microsoft platform expertise to programmes already running. Primes engage us as a subcontractor where the work demands skills, clearances or sovereign positioning their broader delivery model can’t supply at speed.

This positioning is deliberate. It means our incentives align with the prime’s delivery outcomes. We don’t bid against partners. We don’t poach scope. We don’t build a parallel customer relationship behind the prime’s back.

How we engage
PARTNERSHIP MODELS
01

Embedded technical depth

Northforge architects and engineers embedded within the prime’s delivery team. We bring the cleared technical practitioners; the prime owns governance and customer relationship.

02

Specialist work package

A defined work package within the broader programme: the IRAP accreditation track, the M365 deployment, the Azure landing zone build. Northforge owns delivery of the package to agreed quality and timeline.

03

Advisory & uplift

Short-form engagements: architecture reviews, accreditation preparation, exception decisions, design-time consultation. Drawn upon by the prime where specialist input adds disproportionate value.

04

Sovereign carve-out

For programmes where part of the work requires Australian-owned delivery (FOCI mitigation, classified handling), Northforge takes that carve-out within the broader consortium structure.

What we bring
WHY PRIMES CHOOSE US

The team that scopes the engagement has personally delivered SECRET-level on-premises Defence environments. We bring instinct, not just methodology — the kind of contextual judgement that primes can’t recruit at scale and consultancies can’t train into junior staff.

The Forge Method™ produces IRAP-ready artefacts as a byproduct of build — not as a separate workstream. For primes running fixed-price Defence contracts, this is the difference between hitting the milestone and explaining why the milestone slipped.

How we don’t engage
WHAT WE WON’T DO
  • We don’t bid against partners.Once we’re in conversation with a prime about a programme, we don’t pursue the same opportunity through a competing consortium.
  • We don’t poach scope.Our remit on a programme is what was scoped at the start. We expand only where the prime asks us to expand — never by routing around them.
  • We don’t build a shadow customer relationship.The customer relationship is the prime’s. We support delivery. We don’t cultivate the end customer behind the prime’s back.
  • We don’t take fixed-price on uncontrolled scope.If scope can’t be defined, we engage time-and-materials with clear governance. Fixed-price only where the work is bounded enough that we can hold it.
